Autonomous Navigation in Dark Unknown Environments using Vision
Supervisor: Dr. Pawel Ladosz   |   Co-supervisor: Prof. Hujun Yin
PhD Student: Nathan Shankar

Robots struggle when the lights go out—cameras fail, maps disappear, and navigation breaks down. This research focuses on giving robots the ability to see and move reliably in complete darkness. By combining infrared sensing, intelligent image enhancement, and spatial awareness, this system enables robots to understand their surroundings even when there’s no visible light.

The significance of this research lies in making vision-based navigation robust and reliable in all lighting conditions, reducing the need for complex sensor arrays and enabling low-cost, vision-only autonomous systems.
